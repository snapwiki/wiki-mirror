The Snap! Forums are forums to discuss about Snap!. They are run with Discourse, a forum software. The forums' URL is https://forum.snap.berkeley.edu/. The developers are regular users of the wiki; they reply to every thread about Snap! and help the users. There are many active users so users' questions are usually answered very fast.

The official Snap! forum topic for the Snap! Wiki can be found here.

Trust Levels

The forums also use trust levels, a Discourse feature which give the users different permissions within the forums. Trust levels can be obtained by being active on the forum. The Snap! forum only has two trust levels; the first one is limited to discourage spambots, but the second one can be achieved (https://forum.snap.berkeley.edu/t/trust-levels/1953/2).

Forum Down for Moderation Plan

The Snap! Forum had been getting a gradually increasing amount of spam, ghostposting, and other posts that were unrelated to Snap! or computer science, or in some way just added noise to the forum. In other words, the "signal to noise ratio had been getting lower". There were also a few accounts of personally identifying information (PII) being posted. On the 18th of October, 2020, the Snap! Forum was down; that is, for all categories with the exception of Meta(, which has since been removed, so now other than Bug Reports > Forum Bugs), new topics and posts cannot be added or replied to. The Uncategorized category has also been removed. The following text could be found inside a banner on every Snap! Forums page:

"The Snap! Forum is temporarily down as of 18 Oct 2020, while we develop a plan for making it work in a way that furthers our goals (building and supporting a community of Snap! programmers and computer science students) without posing an impossible moderation burden, and minimizing the potential for publication of Personally Identifiable Information.

This pause is not meant to be a punishment; we blame ourselves, not you, for allowing the current situation to arise. We hope to welcome you back within a few days."

The forum suspension ended on 24th of October, 2020 and an announcement with new rules for the forum was posted.
